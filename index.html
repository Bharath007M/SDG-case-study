Case Study: SDG Goals 7, 8, and 9

Clean Energy, Decent Work, and Innovation

Introduction

This case study examines three key Sustainable Development Goals:

  • SDG 7: Affordable and Clean Energy
  • SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth
  • SDG 9: Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure

Case Study 1: Morocco's Solar Energy Project

SDG 7 - Clean Energy

Problem:

High dependence on energy imports (97% of energy needs).

Solution:

  • Built Noor Solar Complex (world's largest concentrated solar power plant)
  • Investment in solar and wind energy infrastructure
  • Public-private partnerships for renewable projects

Results:

  • Solar capacity increased to 700 MW by 2020
  • Plans to export clean energy to Europe
  • 500,000+ people gained access to clean electricity

Case Study 2: Germany's Apprenticeship System

SDG 8 - Decent Work

Problem:

High youth unemployment and skills shortage.

Solution:

  • Students work 3-4 days in companies, 1-2 days in school
  • 350 recognized job training programs
  • Apprentices earn while learning

Results:

  • Youth unemployment only 7% (EU average: 15%)
  • 68% of apprentices hired by training companies
  • Strong skilled workforce supporting manufacturing

Case Study 3: South Korea's Digital Infrastructure

SDG 9 - Innovation and Infrastructure

Problem:

Poor internet connectivity in 1990s.

Solution:

  • Massive fiber-optic network investment
  • Government digitalization of services
  • Tech startup support programs

Results:

  • 95% broadband coverage (world's highest)
  • Digital economy: 8% of GDP
  • Global tech companies (Samsung, LG)

Broadband Coverage

How Goals Connect

  • Energy + Jobs: Solar industry created manufacturing and installation jobs
  • Infrastructure + Growth: Digital networks enabled e-commerce and tech sector
  • Innovation + Energy: Technology advances reduced renewable energy costs

Key Success Factors

  1. Government Leadership: Strong policy and consistent investment
  2. Public-Private Partnership: Government vision + private efficiency
  3. Skills Training: Prepare workers for new industries
  4. Long-term Vision: 10-15 year commitment needed

Recommendations

Start With:

  • Basic infrastructure (electricity, internet)
  • Vocational training for local industries
  • Incentives for clean energy investment

For Success:

  • Set clear national targets
  • Link education to job market needs
  • Learn from successful countries

Conclusion

India, Germany, and South Korea show that clean energy, decent work, and innovation work best together. Countries addressing all three simultaneously see faster progress than those focusing on individual goals.

Key insight: Infrastructure enables innovation, clean energy creates jobs, and skilled workers drive growth.
